Power and pistons

The bottom line on power is having it in surplus to tune for terrain and rider preference. It starts with the right piston size for heavy duty and extreme duty applications. Considering our XCO-worthy, heavy duty Motive brake has Code-like power (our World Cup DH and Red Bull Rampage winning brake), and our extreme duty Maven brake adds an additional 50% on top of that, riders get as much power as they can get, without going crazy.

Take the heat. Use it.

Check any SRAM caliper or pad and you won’t find cooling fins or other add-ons aimed at moving heat away from caliper seals. Instead, we rely on design fundamentals that make the necessary biproduct of all friction, heat, an advantage. It starts with the caliper material itself, and includes careful consideration for caliper size, mass and density. But we don’t do any of this to “beat the heat,” because for consistent brake performance, the right amount of heat improves friction coefficients, increasing power.

The smartest seals. Ever.

We may not think about a brake’s internal seals, but they’re one of the most important factors impacting performance, consistency and friction coefficients. Where most brakes use off-the-shelf seals that can adversely affect performance during heat fluctuations, we developed our own proprietary seal for mineral oil use that’s ultra-consistent throughout extreme temperatures. Such unprecedented heat tolerance massively contributes to a consistent lever-feel and bite point no matter how much heat range is encountered. It’s also why you won’t find cooling fins or other heat-dissipation gadgets on SRAM brakes.

High performance and low maintenance

Thanks to our seal design that eliminates its downsides, we leverage the advantages of mineral oil in our mountain brakes. Thanks to its hydrophobic properties, mineral oil won’t take on water over time, drastically reducing the need for maintenance and bleeding. Plus, our mineral oil was co-developed with Maxima Racing Oils to optimize the consistent performance of our seals regardless of temperature and bleed interval. Mineral oil for the win.

Progressive lever feel

SwingLink™ equipped brakes use a custom-shaped “cam” which creates an intentional progressive lever action that builds more power as you pull. We tailor this progressive leverage across each brake model for maximum power in every application. Compared to what’s called a “binary” or “on/off” feel, a progressive lever feel offers superior traction fidelity, gives you more control, and also helps minimize hand fatigue.

Smarter pivot location

By locating the pivot closer to the bar, our lever blade arc matches the natural path of your finger pull, for better feel across the entire stroke. More power. Less fatigue.

Optimal lever shape

Short and perfectly contoured, our lever’s profile and end hook are designed for maximum finger stability and control throughout the pull.

Ambidextrous levers

All aftermarket levers work on either side of the cockpit — so the choice is yours to go “moto” or keep things conventional. Your setup. Your call.

Contact point adjust

A simple contact-point adjustment system – totally decoupled from the reach adjust mechanism. Whether you want more immediate feel with a short throw, or the increased modulation of a long throw. Choose when to engage.

Two pad compounds

Because pad choice is one of the easiest ways to tune your brakes to match your riding, we offer two pad compounds: Organic for maximum bite and quiet operation, and Sintered for the most aggressive sustained braking and toughest weather conditions.

MatchMaker X

All of our brakes pair with SRAM’s tried-and-true cockpit organization system. It’s the ultimate clutter manager, and simplification maker.

Four rotor sizes

With availability in both 6-bolt and Centerlock in 160mm, 180mm, 200mm, and 220mm, the ability to tune power and personalize feel is easy. Plus, setup is quick with our intuitive adaptors.
